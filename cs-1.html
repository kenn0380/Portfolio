Iterating our Problem Statement

The president made it clear that the problems facing the organiztion had nothing to do with donations. They're instead concerned with the engagement and retention of their volunteers and publicity. What could we do, within the scope of our redesign, that would increase return volunteers and public awareness?

The Brain Injury Alliance was designed to raise awareness and enhance the quality of life for all people affected by a brain injury. We have observed that people who donate to local organizations tend to spend ample time researching the organization first; and that the BIA website isnâ€™t set up for easy browsing and pinpointing the content they are looking for. How might we allow individuals and their family members with a TBI who are already overwhelmed to easily find the information they are looking for on the BIA website?

The Solution

  1. Revised Information Architecture and user flows focused on learning more about Brain Injuries
  2. Aesthetic and minimalist design consistent with brand identity, mission, and values
  3. Meaningful media and interactions; strong UX writing to communicate the userâ€™s impact

Our focus was on BIA's goals to educate the community about Brain Injuries and retain their members. If the current content of the site were revised to address everyday users unfamiliar with the diseases, users would more thoroughly understand the condition. Additionally, if users were able to register for events, seeing that others are participating in a legitimate community via the website, BIA would be more likely to establish a personal connection with, and retain its volunteers. Any possible features leading to the possibility of more donations were defined as "low-impact," because more funding for the organization from donors does not indicate increased awareness or volunteer retention.

What worked for users:

  • In usability tests, for example, all participants were able to find more information about brain injuries.

  • One user commented that the overview section on Brain Injuries divides up a large amount of content really well.

What we changed:

  • A participant noted that it would feel more personal if we made the confirmation page after registering for an event more warm and inviting.

  • Users expected to see individual events when hovering over the main menu item "Events."
